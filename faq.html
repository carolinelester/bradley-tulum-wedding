FAQ's

What airport do I fly into?

The easiest way to get to Tulum is to fly into the Cancun Airport (CUN). Tulum is located about an hour and 15 minutes south of Cancun via highway.

How do I get from the airport to Tulum?

There's multiple options outlined in the travel section of the website but the main two are renting a car or taking a shuttle. For cars I recommend Easy Way Rental which is a small local rental business that is straight forward with their cost and fees. I would recommend a 4x4 as some Tulum streets are not fully paved. The other option is to hire a shuttle. Typically you can rent either a private shuttle for up to 10+ people for less than $200 round trip or there's also group shuttles for cheaper.

What currency does Mexico use?

Mexico uses the pesos with the exchange rate of around 22 pesos = 1 USD and 28 pesos = 1 pound.

Where can I get pesos?

If you'd like it's easy to visit your local bank and order pesos before your trip, setting you up well when you arrive. Once you get to Tulum there's banks in the city centers with reliable ATM's. Please tell your bank you're traveling internationally before you get to Mexico to be able to get pesos out. Be cautious when getting pesos out of "random" ATM's as some will be corrupt.

Can I use my credit card in Tulum?

Yes! Most places in Tulum, especially in the resort areas will accept credit cards like Visa and Mastercard. Although, in Tulum it's necessary to always carry pesos for taxis and for some places that do not accept card.

How do roadblocks & toll booths work in Tulum?

If you choose to travel into the Yuchatan or other areas of Mexico you might encounter roadblocks and tollbooths. Here you will have to pay a fee to the locals in cash to pass so always have spare pesos on you for these stops. The roads are safe and people are nice as long as you politely comply.

How do the beach clubs work?

Almost every resort has a beach club. The nicer the beach club the higher the minimum spend. Most are reasonably priced, have a good DJ, and feature cabanas and louging areas for certain minimum spends. If the minimum spend for a cabana is 500 pesos ($22) then you must spend that money on food and drinks while you are there to continue staying there.

Can I get phone service in Tulum?

If you're an American on Verizon with an unlimited plan, using your phone in Mexico should be completely free. AT&T normally allows for 1G of free data in Mexico. There's service in town and most of the beach, along with wifi pretty much everywhere you go. There are dead spots for LTE coverage on certain parts of the beach.

Can I drink the water in Tulum?

Do not drink the tap water in Tulum unless you plan to sit on the toilet all vacation. Pretty much everywhere you will be going knows this and therefore will serve you bottled waters at meals, etc.
