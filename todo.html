things to do

What isn't there to do in Tulum? We recommend checking out all the natural caves (cenotes), the ancient Mayan Ruins, the beautiful beaches, the snorkling or scuba and modern art all around Tulum.

Beach Clubs

Beach Clubs are a great way to lounge on the beach while eating and drinking. Essentially, these are resorts that allow outsiders to use their beach and restaurants. Some are free, while other's require a minimum spend to use the facilities.

Mayan Ruins

There's multiple Ruins around Tulum, the most famous being the Mayan Ruins right next to the ocean at the top of the archological zone.


Cenotes

A cenote is a deep, water-filled sinkhole in limestone that is created when the roof of an underground cavern collapses. This creates a natural pool which is then filled by rain and water flowing from underground rivers. The word cenote comes from the Mayan word dzonot, which means "well." Cenotes tend to have very clear, cool, fresh water.

Spa Day

I've heard multiple people rave about the Mayan Clay Spa in Tulum. If you need some pampering check out the Mayan Clay Body Massage or maybe the Sea Salt Exfoliation.


Rent a Boat

Explore the coastline with a boat rental! All charters include crew, open bar, gourmet meals, and snorkeling equipment. You can schedule the boats from 4-8 hours or even overnight to explore the ocean or Cozumel. The boats take off from a marina just north of Tulum!

Scuba or Snorkle

In Tulum you can snorkle or scuba inside certain cenotes or at Tulum's reef. You can also take a day trip to Cozumel and explore their expansive reefs.
