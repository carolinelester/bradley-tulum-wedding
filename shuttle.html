shuttles

If you'd rather not drive during your trip, it makes the most sense to you to arrive in Tulum via shuttle from the Cancun Airport. There's private shuttles, taxis or public buses depending on your price range. Once you reach Tulum, you can travel via taxi, rental car or bike to your destination.

Shuttles

Shuttles are great to get your family or friend group from the Cancun Airport to your place of stay in Tulum. A shuttle can be as cheap at $10 a person for the hour and half travel to Tulum with up to 16 people. This is also a very safe option. I recommend prebooking your shuttle before arriving in Cancun. You can get shuttles one way or purchase them for your roundtrip.

Taxi's

Taxi's might be the quickest option of getting to Tulum right after you land in the Cancun airport but most likely will be the most expensive. I've seen reports on blogs that they can be as cheap as $30 a person for the transfer but it seems more likely to be around $100 for a trip into town. To take a taxi you must purchase a ticket from an approved vendor at the airport and then ride with the taxi company.

Public Buses

The ADO public Buses are definitely your cheapest option! While they can take the longest of any of our travel options it can save you lots of money getting to Tulum. There's no direct bus to Tulum but you can take the route that has a transfer in Playa Del Carmen. Once in Playa del Carmen then you can hop on the bus to Tulum. The buses should leave every 30 minutes.
